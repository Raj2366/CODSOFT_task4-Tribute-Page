Mother Teresa

A nun who became the saint


Mother Marry Teresa Bojaxhiu,honored in catholic church as Saint Teresa of Calcuta.

Mother Mary Teresa (real name was Anjeze Gonxhe) was born on 26 August 1910 on Skopje (now the capital of North Macedonia). Her father name is Nikolle Bojaxhiu who was a politician and mother name is Dranafile Bojaxhiu. She was a brother and sister named as Lazar Bojaxhiu and Aga Bojaxhiu. Her father was died in 1919 due to some disease when she was only eight year old. When Teresa was 18 year old, she left home and joined the Sisters of Loreto in Rathfarnham, Irelandafter that she never meet her family again. After spending a year in Ireland, she transferred to the same convent at Darjeeling, India. She died on 5th September 1997 at the age of 87 in Calcutta, west Bengal, india. In the year 1950, mother teresa founded the Missionaries of Charity which is basically a Roman Catholic religious congregation that had more than 4,500 nuns and was currently active in around 133 countries in the year 2012 relatively. The congregation especially manages homes for people who are suffering from HIV/AIDS, leprosy and tuberculosis. It also runs mobile clinics, soup kitchens, dispensaries, children’s and family counselling programmes, as well as orphanages and school for them accordingly. Teresa received a number of awards, such as the year 1962 Ramon Magsaysay Peace Prize and 1979 Nobel Peace Prize. She was canonised on 4 September 2016, and the anniversary of her death is her feast day. Teresa was admired by many for her charitable work. She was praised and criticized on various counts, such as for her views on abortion and contraception, and was criticized for poor conditions in her houses for the dying. On 6 September 2017, Teresa and St. Francis Xavier were named co-patrons of the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Calcutta.
